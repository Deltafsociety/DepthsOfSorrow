# Philosophy of DepthsOfSorrow

## Overview

DepthsOfSorrow is a response to the growing reality of digital blackouts, censorship, and controlled connectivity. It exists to defend a simple idea: access to information is a fundamental human right, not a privilege granted by systems of power.

We build tools for resilience, privacy, and continuity of communication in environments where the internet is restricted, fragmented, or deliberately degraded.

This project is not about aggression. It is about endurance.

---

## Core Belief

We believe that:

- The internet should remain open, global, and interoperable
- Individuals have the right to communicate without surveillance or restriction
- Knowledge and expression should not be gated by geography or politics
- Digital isolation is a form of harm when imposed at scale

---

## Mission

DepthsOfSorrow exists to:

- Support access to the global internet during outages and restrictions
- Design resilient connectivity tools that prioritize privacy and safety
- Strengthen user autonomy in hostile or unstable network environments
- Reduce dependency on centralized gatekeepers of information

---

## Principles

### 1. Privacy First
We design systems that minimize data exposure. We do not collect unnecessary user data, and we assume hostile network environments by default.

### 2. Resilience Under Pressure
Our tools are built for failure scenarios: partial connectivity, filtering, throttling, and surveillance. If the network is broken, we assume it will remain useful anyway.

### 3. Minimal Trust
We reduce trust requirements wherever possible. Users should not need to trust us, third parties, or centralized infrastructure to use the system.

### 4. Accessibility
Technology must remain usable under stress. Interfaces and systems should be simple enough to operate even in low-bandwidth or degraded conditions.

### 5. Ethical Use
We do not promote harm, violence, or exploitation. Our focus is communication continuity and digital survival, not disruption or attack.

---

## Threat Model (High-Level)

We assume environments where:

- Internet access may be partially or fully restricted
- Traffic may be monitored or filtered
- Infrastructure may be unstable or intentionally degraded
- Users may face legal or social risk for connectivity attempts

We design accordingly, focusing on robustness rather than reliance on ideal conditions.

---

## What We Are Not

DepthsOfSorrow is not:

- A tool for offensive cyber operations
- A system for bypassing law enforcement investigation
- A platform for distributing malicious software
- A mechanism for harassment or coordinated harm

We are building infrastructure for communication survival, not escalation.

---

## Responsibility

Technology does not exist in a vacuum. Tools that increase access can also increase risk if misused.

We acknowledge this and commit to:

- Avoiding unnecessary data retention
- Limiting attack surface and abuse vectors
- Prioritizing user safety over performance or reach
- Being transparent about design choices where possible
